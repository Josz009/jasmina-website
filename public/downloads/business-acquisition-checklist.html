Business Acquisition Due Diligence Checklist

A comprehensive 47-point checklist for evaluating any business before purchase

I. Financial Analysis (12 Items)

  • Review 3-5 years of audited financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow)
  • Analyze revenue trends — growth rate, seasonality, and consistency
  • Identify revenue concentration — what % comes from top 5 customers?
  • Review gross and net profit margins year-over-year
  • Examine cash flow from operations vs. reported net income
  • Calculate key financial ratios: current ratio, debt-to-equity, ROE, ROA
  • Review accounts receivable aging — how much is 60+ days overdue?
  • Analyze working capital requirements and seasonal fluctuations
  • Identify one-time income or expenses that distort profitability
  • Review owner compensation and personal expenses running through the business
  • Examine capital expenditure history and upcoming requirements
  • Verify tax returns match financial statements for past 3 years

II. Legal & Compliance (8 Items)

  • Review all pending or threatened litigation
  • Examine all contracts: customer, vendor, lease, partnership agreements
  • Verify all licenses, permits, and regulatory compliance
  • Review intellectual property: trademarks, patents, copyrights
  • Check for environmental liabilities or obligations
  • Review employment agreements, non-competes, and NDAs
  • Verify corporate structure and ownership documentation
  • Check for any government investigations or regulatory actions

III. Operations (9 Items)

  • Document all key business processes and standard operating procedures
  • Evaluate technology systems, software, and infrastructure
  • Review inventory management and valuation methods
  • Assess supply chain dependencies and vendor concentration
  • Evaluate physical assets: condition, maintenance history, replacement timeline
  • Review quality control processes and customer complaint history
  • Assess capacity utilization — how much room for growth without new investment?
  • Review insurance coverage: property, liability, key person, business interruption
  • Identify any critical single points of failure in operations

IV. Human Resources (7 Items)

  • Review organizational chart and key employee roles
  • Assess key person dependency — would the business survive if any one person left?
  • Review compensation structure, benefits, and any unfunded obligations
  • Examine employee turnover rates and hiring history
  • Review any pending HR issues, complaints, or disputes
  • Assess management team capability and willingness to stay post-acquisition
  • Review any union agreements or collective bargaining obligations

V. Market & Competitive Position (6 Items)

  • Analyze industry trends, growth prospects, and potential disruptions
  • Identify direct and indirect competitors and their market positions
  • Assess the business's competitive advantages and barriers to entry
  • Review customer satisfaction data, online reviews, and brand reputation
  • Evaluate market share trends over the past 3-5 years
  • Identify emerging threats: new competitors, regulatory changes, technology shifts

VI. Valuation & Deal Structure (5 Items)

  • Calculate normalized EBITDA after adjustments for owner compensation, one-time items
  • Apply multiple valuation methods: earnings multiple, DCF, asset-based, comparable transactions
  • Assess goodwill and intangible asset values
  • Evaluate deal structure: asset purchase vs. stock purchase, seller financing, earn-outs
  • Plan for post-acquisition integration: first 90 days, first year, first 3 years
Important: This checklist is a starting framework, not a substitute for professional analysis. Every business is unique, and a thorough due diligence process should be tailored to the specific opportunity. I recommend working with an independent financial analyst who can dig deeper into the areas most critical to your particular acquisition.
