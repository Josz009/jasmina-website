Financial Ratios Quick Reference Card

20 essential ratios every business owner and investor should know

Liquidity Ratios

Measure a company's ability to meet short-term obligations.

RatioFormulaHealthy RangeWhat It Tells You
Current RatioCurrent Assets / Current Liabilities1.5 - 3.0Can the company pay its bills due within a year?
Quick Ratio(Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities1.0 - 2.0Same as above, but excludes inventory (harder to liquidate)
Cash RatioCash / Current Liabilities0.5 - 1.0Most conservative — can the company pay bills with cash on hand?
Working CapitalCurrent Assets - Current LiabilitiesPositiveThe dollar amount of short-term financial cushion

Profitability Ratios

Measure how efficiently a company generates profit.

RatioFormulaVaries ByWhat It Tells You
Gross Profit Margin(Revenue - COGS) / RevenueIndustryHow much profit remains after direct costs of production
Operating MarginOperating Income / RevenueIndustryProfit after all operating expenses (before interest and tax)
Net Profit MarginNet Income / RevenueIndustryBottom-line profit as % of revenue — the "real" margin
Return on Equity (ROE)Net Income / Shareholders' Equity15%+ is strongHow effectively the company uses shareholder investment
Return on Assets (ROA)Net Income / Total Assets5%+ is decentHow efficiently the company uses all its assets to generate profit
EBITDA MarginEBITDA / RevenueIndustryOperating profitability before non-cash charges and financing

Leverage Ratios

Measure how much debt a company uses to finance operations.

RatioFormulaHealthy RangeWhat It Tells You
Debt-to-EquityTotal Debt / Shareholders' Equity< 2.0How much the company relies on debt vs. equity financing
Debt-to-AssetsTotal Debt / Total Assets< 0.6What portion of the company's assets are financed by debt
Interest CoverageEBIT / Interest Expense> 3.0Can the company comfortably pay interest on its debt?

Efficiency Ratios

Measure how well a company manages its operations.

RatioFormulaBetter WhenWhat It Tells You
Inventory TurnoverCOGS / Average InventoryHigherHow many times inventory is sold and replaced per year
Days Sales Outstanding(Accounts Receivable / Revenue) x 365LowerAverage days to collect payment from customers
Asset TurnoverRevenue / Total AssetsHigherHow efficiently the company uses its assets to generate revenue
Accounts Payable TurnoverCOGS / Average Accounts PayableModerateHow quickly the company pays its own suppliers

Valuation Ratios

Used to assess whether a stock or business is fairly priced.

RatioFormulaContextWhat It Tells You
Price-to-Earnings (P/E)Share Price / Earnings Per ShareCompare to peersHow much investors pay per dollar of earnings
EV/EBITDAEnterprise Value / EBITDACompare to peersBusiness valuation independent of capital structure
Price-to-Book (P/B)Share Price / Book Value Per Share< 1 may be undervaluedMarket value vs. accounting book value
Important: No single ratio tells the full story. Always analyze ratios in context: compare to industry peers, look at trends over 3-5 years, and consider the business's specific circumstances. A ratio that looks "bad" in isolation may be perfectly reasonable for a particular industry or growth stage.
